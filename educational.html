When is an Emergency a True Emergency?

 

If your pet is experiencing a life-threatening emergency, contact your nearest veterinary hospital at soon as possible. Be prepared before traveling to know locations of veterinary clinics that may be in your area and the availability of after hours care. Emergencies are never expected.

 

If your pet is experiencing any of the following conditions seek immediate medical attention:

 

• Any major traumatic event such as hit by a car, dog fight, penetrating wound
• Difficulty breathing
• Collapse
• Lethargy, not eating (especially if a cat for 1-2 days)
• Persistent vomiting and/or diarrhea
• Distended and/or painful abdomen
• Seizures, specifically if repeated or continuous
• Straining to urinate or not passing urine
• Ingestion of a toxin
• Nonstop bleeding

 

-Call the emergency clinic ahead of time if your location does not offer 24/7 care.

-Do not give any human medication before speaking to a veterinarian.

-Be very careful handling an injured animal, whether your own, someone else's or wildlife.

-If you are aware of any toxin your pet ingested, whether a chemical or plant, bring with you to the hospital.

-Veterinary hospitals charge a higher fee for after hours, weekends, holiday care. Be prepared financially (see Care Credit link provided below).
